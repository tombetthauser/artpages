Getting Started:

This section contains the basic instructions that you will need to organize the files necessary to get your website running. If you haven't checked out the Information section above you may want to before getting started here.

I need your feedback! The details of these instructions are still being fine-tuned so any questions / feedback regarding sections needing clarification are greatly appreciated via email at tombetthauser@gmail.com (subject heading "AP Feedback").

Hope this is at least semi-enjoyable!



Step 01:

This one's easy, just make a new folder on your computer's desktop. Title it simply with "Your First Name" followed by "Website" (i.e. "Tom's Website" or "Alex's Website" etc).

Step 02:

This one's pretty easy too. Inside your folder, create a numbered folder for every seperately linked page you want to appear on your website, numbering them to indicate which link you would like to appear first, second etc Examples might include:

  • Home / Homepage
  • Images / Portfolio
  • CV / Resume
  • Contact / Links

Feel free to make up your own titles for these folders and add more / fewer if desired, as long as they are numbered in the order you would like them to appear on your website.

Your link names will appear exactly as you have written them in the folder titles (but without the leading number).

Step 03:

If you would like to have a single image appear as your homescreen, open the folder you have labelled "Home," "Home Page" etc. and put the single homescreen image you have chosen in this folder named specifically "Image1.jpg" (this title using no spaces is important).

Please note that the image must be a jpeg between 750 and 2500 pixels in height / width.

Step 04:

This one's a little bit more irritating. You're going to copy images of your work into all the page folders that need them (like your "Images / Portfolio" folder or maybe "New Work" / "Old Work" folders if you made some of those).

The titles you choose for these image files will determine the exact order they appear in on your webpage (i.e. "Image1.jpg" will appear first, "Image2.jpg" will appear second etc). They will not be the titles that appear on your webpage so don't worry about that yet, we'll deal with that in the next step.

Please take your time to make sure the order of these images is exactly the order you want them to appear in on your webpage! Remember the order they'll appear in on your webpage is determined by the "Image1.jpg" "Image2.jpg" (etc) titles you give them.

Again, please be sure all of your images are jpegs between 750 and 2500 pixels in height / width.

Step 05:

All the information that you would like to appear underneath the images of your work (i.e. Title, Size, Materials, Date) should be put in a seperate text or PDF file made in any program you are comfortable using.

You will start at the top of this text document by typing the name of the first image file in the folder (i.e. "Image1.jpg") and follow this in a seperate paragraph by the image information exactly as you would like it to appear on your website, including spaces, dashes, quotes, punctuation, capitalization, italicization etc.

You will need to make a seperate text document for every folder that has images in it. If this text file is not included in a folder, the assumption will be that your work should have no accompanying information, which may be the case on pages like your "Home" or "Home Page."

Step 06:

If you would like to have a linked page with your resume information first make sure you have a folder labelled "CV" or "resume" or "Artist Info." or anything else you'd like to call this link.

In this folder you'll put another text or PDF file with all the resume information you'd like to be displayed using the simplest formatting possible (avoid fancy fonts, indentations, columns, bullet-pointed lists etc. as they wont carry over to your page well.

Keep it as simple as possible! That being said, if the formatting in your resume document doesn't translate well onto your webpage it will just be made available as a clickable link to a seperate PDF, which will be the exact PDF file you copy into this folder.

Step 07:

Following the same process as Step 06 you can add other text-based pages like "Contact" or "Links" or anything else you think would be useful. Just make a new numbered folder and add another simply formatted text or PDF document.

Also optional here is adding any images you might like to include such as an image of you in your studio etc. Just follow the same image process as before, naming your image(s) "Image1.jpg" etc. to determine the order they appear in (if there are multiples) and checking to make sure they are jpegs between 750 and 2500 pixels.

Step 08:

This step is important! Once you think you're done adding pages, images and text documents go through the following checklist to make sure you're ready to send all of this. Once you send it and your website gets built you'll have to wait for a medium-large sized update if you want to change anything which is somewhat involved and takes time.


Double Check List:

Check your folder numbers! They will determine their order on your webpage. i.e. "1) Home Page" will be the first link that appears near your name on your site followed by "2) Images / Portfolio" etc.

Check your image numbers! They will determine their order on whichever page / folder they appear in. i.e. "Image1.jpg" will appear at the top of a page followed by "Image2.jpg" etc. They need to be in this format with zeros and no spaces.

Check your image format & sizes! All images need to be jpegs between 750 and 2500 pixels in height / width.

Check your image information! Make sure you have the image information that you want to appear under each image in the right order, formatted the way you want in the right text file or PDF, it should be in the same folder as the image file.

Step 09:

Alright, are you really done? Are you sure? You read that whole checklist right? Alright then, you're ready to compress your folder and email it off to turn into a website!

If you've never done this it's pretty simple, just click on the folder once with your mouse, then locate the "Compress" option either in the top menu or in the menu that appears when you second-click the folder with your mouse.

After you select "Compress" a new file should appear near your folder with the same basic name, this is it's compressed version which should be small enough to email.

Step 10:

Now all you have to do is email your compressed folder. Please title the email with the same name as your folder (i.e. "Tom's Website" or "Alex's Website") and then attach the compressed version of your folder. It should be the one ending with ".zip".

Step 11:

There is no step 11, you're done! Just sit back, relax and wait for an email about something you did wrong. Exciting!

If you still need something to do I guess you could throw away that .zip file on your desktop, you wont need it again. Also make sure you keep your website folder somewhere safe and don't change anything in it until you're ready to do a medium-large sized update. Seperate instructions coming on that soon.

Thanks for that work, I'm looking forward to putting all your sites together!
